Assignment: Literary Themes Website
Course: English 6
 Subjects: English & Web Development
Task Overview
In this assignment, you will create a website that explores key themes in classic literature. The website will support your spoken final task in English, where you analyze a literary work and connect it to modern society.
The focus in English 6 is on:
      * Artificial Intelligence & Ethics/Relationships
 Frankenstein (Mary Shelley)



Website Content Requirements
Your website should include:
         1. Introduction

            * Present the theme and literary work(s)
            * Find and present basic facts about the author.
            * Why is (or is not) this author typical of the period when he/she worked?
            * What impact did he/she have on his/her contemporary society? In what ways has his/her writing influenced his/her followers?
            * What are our views of him/her today? What is your opinion?
            * Have you seen this author being referred to? In what situations? (e.g. in reviews of other novels, reviews of films, films based on the work of this author, music etc.)

            * Explain the historical context

            * Introduce the main question or idea

               2. Analysis

                  * Analyze how the theme is presented in the text

                  * Use examples, quotations, or references

                  * Explain the author’s message and perspective

                     3. Connection to Modern Society

                        * Compare the historical context with today’s society

                        * Discuss similarities and differences

                        * Reflect on why the theme is still relevant

English 6: Spoken Final Task (Mandatory)
The final product in English is a spoken assignment, based on your website.
Choose one format:
Option 1: Presentation / Pitch
                           * 4–6 minutes per student

                           * Structured and analytical

                           * Use your website as visual support

                           * Focus on explaining and comparing past and present

Option 2: Spoken Reflection
                              * 4–6 minutes per student

                              * More personal and reflective

                              * Discuss the theme’s relevance today

                              * Include your own interpretations and conclusions

Your spoken task must:
                                 * Be clear and well-structured

                                 * Show analysis and reflection (English 6 level)

                                 * Use varied vocabulary and appropriate academic language

                                 * Make clear connections between literature and contemporary society
